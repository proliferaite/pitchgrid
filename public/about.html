About PitchGrid

PitchGrid started the way most useful tools do - out of necessity. As a softball dad watching my daughter's games, I kept wishing I could sync up multiple video angles to really understand what was happening in those crucial moments.

The Problem That Started It All

Picture this: You're recording your kid's at-bat from the stands while another parent films from behind home plate. Later, you want to see both angles of that perfect swing, but trying to manually sync them up is a nightmare. Frame rates are different, timing is off, and you end up spending more time fighting with video files than actually analyzing the play.

That frustration led to a simple question: Why isn't there an easy way to sync multiple videos and analyze them side by side?

What PitchGrid Does

PitchGrid solves the video synchronization problem that coaches and athletes face every day. Instead of juggling multiple video files and trying to line them up manually, you can:

  • Sync multiple video angles with frame-perfect precision
  • Analyze performance from different perspectives simultaneously
  • Share synchronized video grids with players and parents
  • Focus on coaching instead of fighting with technology

The key insight: Most video analysis tools are either too complex for youth sports or too simple to be useful. PitchGrid hits the sweet spot - powerful enough for serious analysis, simple enough for busy coaches.

Beyond Sports

While PitchGrid was built for sports, users have found creative applications we never imagined. Dance instructors sync rehearsal footage, researchers analyze animal behavior, and musicians coordinate performance videos. The core need - understanding what happens when multiple perspectives come together - spans way beyond the baseball diamond.

Built by Real Users

Every feature in PitchGrid comes from real feedback from coaches, athletes, and parents who use it regularly. We don't add complexity for its own sake. If it doesn't help you understand performance better or save time, it doesn't go in the app.

The development process is straightforward: we build what people actually need, test it with real users, and iterate based on how it performs in actual games and practices.
