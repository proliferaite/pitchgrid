Quarterback Mechanics

The quarterback position demands precise mechanics for accuracy, power, and consistency. Proper throwing technique is the foundation of effective quarterback play.

Stance & Setup

  • Feet shoulder-width apart under center
  • Knees slightly bent, weight balanced
  • Hands positioned correctly for snap
  • Eyes scanning pre-snap reads

Drop Back Technique

  • First step with outside foot (right for right-handed QB)
  • Maintain pocket presence and balance
  • Keep shoulders square to line of scrimmage
  • Ball secured in two hands during drop

Throwing Motion

  • High elbow position during wind-up
  • Step toward target with front foot
  • Hip and shoulder rotation in sequence
  • Follow through with wrist snap
Video Analysis Tip: Film from behind and to the side of the quarterback to capture both footwork and arm motion. Use slow motion to analyze the throwing sequence and timing.

Common Quarterback Errors

  • Dropping elbow below shoulder level
  • Stepping away from pressure instead of up in pocket
  • Inconsistent release point
  • Poor footwork timing with receivers

Receiver Route Running

Precise route running creates separation and timing in the passing game. Every route requires specific techniques for maximum effectiveness.

Route Fundamentals

  • Sharp cuts at break points
  • Consistent depth and timing
  • Proper hand placement for catches
  • Eyes tracking ball throughout route

Release Techniques

  • Quick first step off the line
  • Hand fighting to avoid press coverage
  • Head fakes and body positioning
  • Acceleration through the break

Catching Technique

  • Hands extended away from body
  • Eyes track ball into hands
  • Secure catch before turning upfield
  • Protect ball through contact
Video Analysis Tip: Film routes from multiple angles - end zone view for route depth and sideline view for separation. Focus on the break point and catch technique.

Defensive Fundamentals

Sound defensive technique starts with proper stance, footwork, and positioning. These fundamentals apply across all defensive positions.

Defensive Stance

  • Feet shoulder-width apart
  • Knees bent, weight on balls of feet
  • Hands ready in athletic position
  • Eyes on offensive player's hips

Tackling Technique

  • Head up, eyes on target
  • Drive through hips and legs
  • Wrap arms around ball carrier
  • Drive feet through contact

Coverage Principles

  • Maintain proper depth and leverage
  • Keep eyes on receiver and quarterback
  • Break on ball at highest point
  • Communicate with teammates

Common Defensive Errors

  • Poor tackling form leading to missed tackles
  • Getting caught flat-footed in coverage
  • Overrunning plays due to poor angles
  • Lack of communication in zone coverage

Video Analysis Camera Angles

Effective football analysis requires multiple camera angles to capture the complexity of the game.

Quarterback Analysis

  • Behind QB: Footwork and pocket movement
  • Side view: Throwing motion and mechanics
  • End zone: Field vision and decision making

Receiver Analysis

  • Sideline view: Route running and separation
  • End zone: Route depth and timing
  • Behind QB: Release and catch technique

Defensive Analysis

  • Elevated view: Formation and alignment
  • Field level: Technique and fundamentals
  • End zone: Coverage and pursuit angles
